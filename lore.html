# Lore

Every community has a story. Here's how three students with an idea became a multi-year, multi-domain tech community.

In 2019, three engineering students sat down with a simple frustration — there was no real space on campus for people who wanted to learn beyond the syllabus. No shortcuts, no spoon-feeding, just curiosity and people helping each other grow. That frustration became Point Blank.

2019 · Chapter One

Three Students, One Idea

Mohit Agarwal, Soumya Pattanayak, and Ashutosh Pandey started Point Blank as a small group chat for solving competitive programming problems together. What began as shared frustration over hard problems turned into a habit of showing up for each other, every single day.

2020 · Chapter Two

The First PB Hustle

What started as informal problem-sharing turned into the club's first organised contest — PB Hustle. A handful of members competed for bragging rights. The leaderboard became something people actually cared about.

2021 · Chapter Three

Open Source Enters the Chat

Ashutosh's GSoC selection with Arduino changed the club's trajectory. Suddenly, "open source" wasn't an abstract term — it was something a member of your own group chat had actually done. The OSS wing was born.

2022–2023 · Chapter Four

Hackathon Season

Point Blank started running its own internal Smart India Hackathon to prep teams for the real thing. The bet paid off — national qualifications became routine, and the club's hackathon culture took shape.

2024 · Chapter Five

From a Group Chat to a Community

With 100+ active members, the club formalised into domains — CP, OSS, Web Dev, ML, Cybersecurity, and Systems. PBCTF launched. PB Chronicles workshops became a monthly fixture. The founders graduated, but the culture stayed.

2026 · Chapter Six

The Biggest Year Yet

13 GSoC mentees. 3 mentors. Multiple SIH finals. The community that started as a group chat for solving hard problems together is now a full-fledged pipeline into the open source and tech world.

"We never set out to build a club. We just didn't want to struggle alone."

— Mohit Agarwal, Co-founder

Fun Facts

💬

It started as a group chat

Point Blank didn't begin with a website or a logo — just a WhatsApp group of three people sharing CP problems.

🏷️

The name came from CP

"Point Blank" was originally the name the founders used for their private contest practice sessions — it just stuck.

🌙

Most contests happen at night

True to CP culture, PB Hustle rounds are almost always scheduled for 9 PM onwards — peak focus hours, apparently.

🎯

No formal recruitment for years

For the first two years, there was no application process — you just had to show up consistently to be "in."

🐍

The Discord bot has a personality

The community's Discord bot has roasted more failed submissions than any human member ever has.

🔁

Every founder still mentors

Despite being years into their careers, all three founders still show up for GSoC orientation sessions every year.
